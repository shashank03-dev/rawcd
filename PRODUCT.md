# Product

## Register

product

## Users

RawCD is for people preserving personal optical media: home-video collectors, families with aging discs, archivists, and technical users who want a local conversion desk rather than a cloud upload flow. They use it while a USB CD/DVD drive is attached, often with uncertain disc formats and imperfect media.

## Product Purpose

RawCD reads personal, unprotected CD/DVD video media, classifies the disc, converts detected clips to MP4, and reports repair work clearly. Success means the user can insert a disc, understand what was found, start a faithful conversion, and trust the output path and repair notes.

## Brand Personality

Typographic, exacting, restorative. The interface should feel like an editorial correction desk for media: calm, structured, color-rich in the places that carry meaning, and precise about what the engine is doing.

## Anti-references

RawCD should not look like generic SaaS marketing, a neon terminal security tool, or a toy retro CD player. Avoid gradient panels, soft card grids, novelty nostalgia, decorative dark-mode drama, vague startup copy, and oversized hero styling.

## Design Principles

- Lead with readable structure: typography and alignment do more work than decoration.
- Treat conversion as evidence: show source, clips, status, warnings, and output paths with clear hierarchy.
- Use color as notation: primary actions, repair states, warnings, and selected sources get deliberate color; inactive surfaces stay quiet.
- Keep the app local and trustworthy: copy names real tools and real constraints.
- Prefer professional familiarity over surprise: standard controls, visible focus, predictable task flow.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA as the baseline. Preserve keyboard-visible focus, readable contrast, reduced-motion behavior, color-blind-safe state differences, and text that wraps cleanly in constrained desktop windows.
